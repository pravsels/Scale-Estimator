How this is computed & what's real vs estimated

In text every word counts. In 30 fps video, frame two is almost a copy of frame one, so counting image patches like words gives a huge, meaningless number. The novelty slider is your estimate of how much of each frame is actually new: text ~100%, a moving robot arm maybe ~5%, near-static video ~2%.

Math: a frame is (res/patch)² × views vision tokens. Effective count keeps the first frame whole and only the new fraction after. State and actions count every step; the instruction counts once. Compute (in FLOPs) is roughly 6 × parameters × tokens × epochs, the Chinchilla rule of thumb, where the 6 is the FLOPs spent per parameter per token. Compared to the ~3.1e23 FLOPs it took to train GPT-3.
